Hybrid Prostheses

Hybrid Devices

Some users may prefer to have a life-like device that still gives them the opportunity to show off their personality or interests. Hybrid devices are a cross between life-like devices and expressive devices. These devices are usually match closely to the user’s skin tone but also have expressive qualities such as tattoos or painted nails. When designing a device, implementing things such as tattoos or giving the user the option to get their nails painted helps with device retention as it can be a human-like aesthetic quality that still allows them to express themselves.

Does Movement Play a Role in Cosmesis?

"Cosmesis stretches beyond the
static form of a prosthesis into the cosmesis of both prosthetic and integrated holistic body movement" (Ritchie et Al, 2011)

Myoelectric devices play a role in cosmesis and help with user retention as it allows the user to fit it with society. The way the device and the user are able to integrate with each other can be classified as a form of cosmesis. Myoelectric devices are designed to closely mimic natural motions. Compared to previous technology, newer myoelectric devices are able to closely mimic human movement. According to Ritchie et al. (2011) it was found that more users prefer electrical cosmetic devices for social outings. They were especially favoured for social outings especially when the user is looking to fit in with everyday tasks.
